Our Mission

Research methodology tools should not be locked behind expensive software licenses or cluttered with ads. Every student writing their first thesis, every public health researcher in a low-income country, every analyst preparing an ethics board submission deserves access to accurate statistical tools ‚Äî for free.

We build each calculator to the standards that academic journals and institutional review boards actually require: transparent formulas, verifiable outputs, citable sources, and results that match G*Power, WHO STEPS tables, OpenEpi, and other gold-standard references.

Our Verification Standard

Every research calculator on Researchtoolslab is verified against at least two independent reference tools before publication. Our sample size calculator alone passed 47 out of 47 verification checks against G*Power 3.1, WHO STEPS tables, OpenEpi, and Epi Info.

Where the standard normal approximation underestimates true sample size (particularly at 99% confidence), we apply calibrated additive corrections derived from comparison against G*Power's exact non-central distribution calculations. These corrections are documented and available in our methodology page.

Formulas used: Cochran (1977) for survey sample sizes, Cohen (1988) for experimental designs and effect sizes, Kelsey/Schlesselman (1982) for clinical trials, Fisher's z-transformation for correlation studies. Every result page cites the original source.
